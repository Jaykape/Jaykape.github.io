Dynamo

One of the frustrations for film photographers is the lack of widely available, single-hand-use film cameras that meet the performance and quality standards of traditional SLR cameras (interchangeable lenses, precision focusing, and full manual control).

Most second-hand options are either broken or prone to breaking easily, and there are very few technicians available to repair them. In most cases, we have to wait a long time for repairs to be completed.

The goal of Dynamo project is to create an open-source, 3D-printable blueprint for a fully functional film camera that is easy to assemble, disassemble, and repair with replaceable parts.

While brands like Lomography offer plastic DIY cameras such as the Konstruktor SLR, these options have significant limitations, particularly when it comes to compatibility with lenses and flashes.
